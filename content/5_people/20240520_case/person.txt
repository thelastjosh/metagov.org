Bio:

Amber Case studies the interactions between humans and technology, and how technology affects culture.

Case is an internationally recognized design advocate and speaker, and the author of four books, including (link: https://www.amazon.com/Calm-Technology-Principles-Patterns-Non-Intrusive/dp/1491925884/ text: Calm Technology) and (link: https://akidsbookabout.com/products/a-kids-book-about-technology text: A Kids Book About Technology). She spent two years as a fellow at MIT’s Center for Civic Media and Harvard’s Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society and was a (link: https://foundation.mozilla.org/en/what-we-fund/fellowships-and-awards-directory/ text: 2021 Mozilla Fellow).

Named one of Inc. Magazine’s 30 under 30 and Fast Company’s Most Influential Women in Technology, she was named a National Geographic Emerging Explorer in 2012 and received the Claude Shannon Innovation Award from Bell Labs. She was the co-founder and CEO of Geoloqi, a location-based software company acquired by Esri.

Case is the founder of (link: https://calmtech.institute/ text: The Calm Tech Institute), a project that exists to establish new standards for designing harmonious human-tech interactions that improve our lives. She works on design, governance, and AI through her position as a Research Director at Metagov.
